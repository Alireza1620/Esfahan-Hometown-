Khoresht Mas

History

Khoresht Mas or Khoresht Mast is a native food of Isfahan. This dish is prepared from condensed yogurt, sheep neck meat, saffron, sugar, and egg yolk, and a little barberry, almond slices, walnuts, and sometimes rose water are added to it. This stew is served cold. This helium-like and elastic food is nowadays consumed as a dessert.

This dish is usually prepared with sheep neck meat, but some use chicken meat. Also, some prepare it in Indian style and use salt and pepper instead of sugar and rose water.

There is not much information about the history of yogurt stew, but it has been seen in the luxurious and big parties of Isfahan for at least a century. Yogurt stew was a main dish in the old days, but nowadays it is used as a dessert.

Indian Khoresht Mast

In Indian style, salt and pepper are used and sugar and rose water are not used. The meat is fried with oil, fried onions and salt are added to it and boiled. Then food spices including ground saffron, a little salt and pepper, cumin and chopped coriander are added to it. Yogurt is stirred until it becomes smooth and after boiling the spiced meat for a few times, it is added to the stew and mixed. After adding yogurt to the stew, the stew should not be boiled and it is enough that the yogurt is heated.
